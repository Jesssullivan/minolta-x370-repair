Multiple sellers list drop-in split-image screens marketed for "X-700 X-500 X-300 X370":

- One listing tracked at roughly **$8.75 + $4.35 shipping** (marked down from $15) — under the $15 target all-in. <https://www.ebay.com/itm/184339098475>
- "Minolta OEM Split Image Focusing Screen for X-700 X-500 X-300 X370" (genuine OEM, not aftermarket) — check current price. <https://www.ebay.com/itm/176724753779>
- "Genuine Minolta X-700 X-570 X-370 Split Image Focusing Screen, Same Day shipping" — check current price. <https://www.ebay.com/itm/324896739895>
- Micro-prism variants (different focusing aid style, same drop-in form factor) also available, e.g. <https://www.ebay.com/itm/284847931995>

**Note on fit:** Minolta officially sold interchangeable-screen kits only for the X-700/X-500/X-570 (see `docs/bibliography.md` → Rokkorfiles). The X-370 was not marketed with a user-selectable screen system, but it shares the same mirror-box screen frame, and these listings are specifically sold as X-370-compatible drop-in replacements — treat this as a physical-fit repair part, not an official Minolta accessory program.

- Feedback on aftermarket screens is mixed — some buyers report a screen visibly dimmer/inferior to OEM, others report no issue. If brightness matters, prefer the OEM-labeled listings above over generic aftermarket ones.

### 3D-print alternative

No STL specifically modeled for the X-370/X-700 EH-7 turned up. The closest lead is bostwickenator's free **"Minolta 7000/9000 Eyecup"**:

- Printables: <https://www.printables.com/en/model/144431-minolta-70009000-eyecup>
- Thingiverse: <https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:3510545>

**Caveat — likely not a direct fit.** The 7000/9000 are autofocus Maxxum/Dynax bodies from a later Minolta generation with a different eyepiece frame than the manual-focus X-series (X-370/X-700/XG/XD) that the EH-7 mounts to. Check the eyepiece frame dimensions on your camera against the model before printing — it may need rescaling or a redesigned mounting lip, or may not be adaptable at all. Given genuine EH-7 units are already available used for well under $15 (see above), buying is the faster path; printing only makes sense if this is wanted as a project regardless of cost/time.
